# REPLIT AGENT PROMPT — SuperScout: Beginner Mode Coaching Programme

## CONTEXT

SuperScout shows the same full experience to every user — 3 options per decision, fixture tickers, ownership spectrum, upside/risk analysis, vibe commentary. For experienced FPL managers, this is home turf. For someone new, they see all the right elements but don’t know what they mean or why they matter.

Beginner mode is NOT a simplified version of the app. It’s a coaching programme. The user sees the full UI from day one — all 3 options, all the data, all the personality. But for the first 4 gameweeks, SuperScout actively teaches them what they’re looking at, one concept per gameweek, delivered in their vibe voice.

Think of it like a football coach watching a new player’s first few training sessions. The coach doesn’t simplify the drills — they run alongside and explain what’s happening as the player does it.

## TASK

### 1. Add a “New to FPL?” screen during onboarding

On the onboarding flow, after the vibe quiz (Screen 3) and before the email signup (Screen 4), add a simple screen:

**“Is this your first season of FPL?”**
Subtitle: “No worries — SuperScout will coach you through the basics over your first few gameweeks. Same app, same features, just with your coach explaining things as you go.”

Two buttons:

- “Yes, I’m new” — sets beginner flag to true
- “Nah, I know the game” — sets beginner flag to false, moves on

Don’t use a toggle — make it feel like a conversation, not a setting.

### 2. Add beginner mode fields to the users table

Add two fields to the users table in Supabase:

- `is_beginner` (boolean, default false) — set during onboarding
- `beginner_rounds_completed` (integer, default 0) — tracks how many gameweek coaching lessons the user has seen. Counts up to 4. Name is deliberately sport-agnostic (“rounds” not “gameweeks”).
- `beginner_lessons_seen` (text, default ‘’) — comma-separated list of which specific lessons have been shown (e.g. “captain,transfers,fixtures,ownership”) to avoid repeats

### 3. The coaching programme — one concept per gameweek

When `is_beginner = true` AND `beginner_rounds_completed < 4`, show a coaching card at the TOP of the recommendation screen (above the first captain or transfer card). The coaching card teaches one concept per gameweek.

The coaching card is a distinctive UI element — not a modal, not a popup. It’s an inline card that sits in the flow, with a different background colour (use a light indigo or surface-alt shade) so it’s clearly from SuperScout the coach, not from the recommendation engine.

Each coaching card has:

- A small “Coach” label or icon at the top
- A headline (the concept being taught)
- 2-3 sentences explaining the concept in the user’s vibe voice
- A “Got it” dismiss button that collapses the card (it doesn’t come back for this gameweek)

**The four lessons (one per gameweek):**

**Round 1 — “Your Captain Is Your Biggest Decision”**

Appears at the top of the Captain Picker on the user’s first visit.

Expert: “Your captain scores double points. Over a full season, that’s a 200-300 point swing — more than any transfer you’ll make. The three options below are ranked by expected return. The SuperScout Pick is highlighted, but the choice is yours.”

Critic: “See those three options below? One of them gets double points this week. Pick wrong and you’ve handed your mini-league rivals a free head start. No pressure. Actually, plenty of pressure — that’s what makes it fun.”

Fanboy: “YOUR CAPTAIN SCORES DOUBLE!! That means every goal, every assist, every bonus point — DOUBLED. Look at the three options below. The SuperScout Pick is the one I’m most hyped about, but you’re the boss!”

**Round 2 — “Transfers: When To Move and When To Hold”**

Appears at the top of the Transfer Advisor on the user’s second active gameweek.

Expert: “You get one free transfer per week. Unused ones roll over, up to a maximum of 5. Each extra transfer beyond your free ones costs 4 points — which is rarely worth it. The options below include a ‘Hold’ option when standing pat is the smart play. Look at the fixture tickers to see why some moves make more sense than others.”

Critic: “One free transfer per week. Waste it and you wait another week. Take a hit — that’s minus 4 points, by the way — and it better be worth it. The Hold option exists for a reason. Sometimes the best transfer is no transfer. I know that’s boring. I don’t care.”

Fanboy: “Transfer time! You get one free transfer every week — unused ones roll over (up to 5!!). The options below show who to bring in and who to let go. Check the fixture colours — green means EASY. If you see a wall of green next to a player’s name, that’s your sign!”

**Round 3 — “Reading the Fixtures”**

Appears at the top of either screen on the user’s third active gameweek. This one specifically draws attention to the fixture ticker and the traffic-light colours.

Expert: “The coloured pills on each card show the next 5 fixtures. Green means a favourable fixture — FDR 1 or 2. Amber is neutral. Red is difficult — a top defence or a tough away trip. When comparing two players with similar expected points, the one with more green in their fixture run is usually the smarter pick over the coming weeks.”

Critic: “Those coloured pills under each player? That’s their fixture run. Green means easy. Red means pain. If you’re choosing between two players and one has a wall of green while the other is staring down red-red-red, this isn’t a hard decision. You’re welcome.”

Fanboy: “See those coloured pills? That’s the FIXTURE RUN — the next 5 games! Green = easy fixtures = MORE POINTS POTENTIAL! Red = tough games = danger zone. When two players look similar, check the colours. A run of green is basically a cheat code!”

**Round 4 — “Ownership and the Crowd”**

Appears at the top of the Captain Picker on the user’s fourth active gameweek. Draws attention to the ownership spectrum.

Expert: “The spectrum bar shows how many other managers own this player. Captaining a highly-owned player protects your rank — if he scores, everyone benefits. Captaining a low-ownership player is a differential — if he scores, you gain ground because most managers miss out. Neither approach is wrong. High ownership is safer. Low ownership is how you climb.”

Critic: “See that bar that says ‘With the crowd’ and ‘Against the crowd’? That’s telling you whether your captain pick is the same as everyone else’s. Pick the popular option and you stay level if he scores. Pick the unknown and you either rocket up the rankings or crash spectacularly. Your call. Both are valid. Only one is boring.”

Fanboy: “That spectrum bar is SO important! If the dot is on the LEFT (with the crowd), loads of managers benefit when he scores — safe but you won’t gain ground. If the dot is on the RIGHT (against the crowd), almost NOBODY else benefits — a haul here is a RANK ROCKET! High risk, high drama!”

### 4. Tracking which lessons have been shown

- After the user dismisses a coaching card (taps “Got it”), increment `beginner_rounds_completed` by 1 and add the lesson name to `beginner_lessons_seen`
- Only show one coaching card per gameweek — even if the user opens both Captain Picker and Transfer Advisor, they see one lesson per active gameweek
- Check `beginner_lessons_seen` before showing a card to avoid showing the same lesson twice
- The lessons must be shown in order (captain → transfers → fixtures → ownership) regardless of which screen the user opens first

### 5. Graduation celebration at the end of Round 4

When the user dismisses the Round 4 coaching card, show a brief celebration moment — an inline card in the same coaching style:

**Headline:** “You’ve got the basics down”

**Expert:** “Four gameweeks in and you now understand captaincy, transfers, fixtures, and ownership. Everything from here is about sharpening your edge. Your coach will still be here — I’ll just talk to you like someone who knows the game.”

**Critic:** “Four weeks. You now know more than half the managers in your mini-league. Don’t let it go to your head. Actually, do — confidence helps.”

**Fanboy:** “FOUR GAMEWEEKS COMPLETE! You basically have a degree in FPL now! The full SuperScout experience is UNLOCKED — all the data, all the tools, all the HYPE. LET’S GO!”

After dismissing this card, set `is_beginner = false`. No more coaching cards appear automatically.

### 6. “Explain this” icons persist for everyone

After beginner mode ends, add small “?” icons next to these elements on recommendation cards:

- Next to the fixture ticker
- Next to the ownership spectrum
- Next to the confidence badge

Tapping “?” shows the same coaching text as the beginner lesson for that element, in the user’s vibe voice. This is available to ALL users (not just graduates of beginner mode) — experienced users might also want a reminder of what something means.

These icons should be subtle — 14px, text-muted colour, positioned unobtrusively. They should NOT appear during beginner mode (the coaching cards already explain everything — showing both would be redundant).

### 7. Settings toggle

Add a “Coaching mode” toggle in the Settings screen so users can:

- Turn it off early if they already know FPL and accidentally said they were new
- Turn it back on if they want to revisit the lessons (resets `beginner_rounds_completed` to 0 and clears `beginner_lessons_seen`)

### 8. Sport-agnostic notes

- `is_beginner` applies to the user overall for now
- `beginner_rounds_completed` uses “rounds” not “gameweeks” so it works for any sport later
- The coaching content in this prompt is FPL-specific (captaincy, transfers, fixtures, ownership). When a new sport is added, equivalent coaching content will be written for that sport. The coaching card component and the tracking logic are sport-agnostic — only the text content changes per sport.

### 9. Do NOT change anything else

- Do NOT modify the AI generation or prompts
- Do NOT simplify or hide any recommendation cards — beginners see the full 3 options from day one
- Do NOT modify the fixture ticker, ownership spectrum, or card components
- Do NOT modify the pre-generation pipeline, cache, or cross-check logic
- Do NOT remove or change any existing UI elements — beginner mode ADDS coaching cards on top of the existing experience, it doesn’t remove anything

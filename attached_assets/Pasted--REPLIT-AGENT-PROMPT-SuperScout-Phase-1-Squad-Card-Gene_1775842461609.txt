# REPLIT AGENT PROMPT — SuperScout Phase 1: Squad Card Generator

This is the Squad Card feature — a shareable image that shows the user’s gameweek squad with an AI-generated quip in their chosen vibe voice. This is SuperScout’s free marketing engine. Every card shared on social media is an ad for the app. The card must look good enough that people WANT to share it.

**Before you start:** Do not run any destructive git operations (git reset –hard, force pushes, orphan commits) at any point during this task. If you encounter a git problem, stop and explain it to me before doing anything.

-----

## WHAT THE SQUAD CARD IS

A shareable image that a user generates after a gameweek. It shows:

1. **The SuperScout brand** — logo or wordmark at the top. Subtle but always visible. Every share is brand awareness.
1. **The user’s team name** — pulled from the FPL API (the user’s fantasy team name, not their real name).
1. **The gameweek number** — e.g. “Gameweek 12”
1. **Their starting 11 in formation** — player surnames arranged in the actual formation they played (e.g. 4-4-2, 3-5-2). Show each player’s points scored that gameweek next to their name. Captain marked with (C), vice-captain with (V). Highlight the captain visually.
1. **Their bench** — 4 bench players listed below the formation with their points. If any bench player was auto-subbed in, mark them visually.
1. **Total gameweek points** — large and prominent.
1. **Rank movement** — “↑ 52,341” (green) or “↓ 18,209” (red) or “—” (no change). Show their new overall rank.
1. **The AI quip** — a one-liner generated by the AI in the user’s chosen vibe voice. This is the share trigger. Examples:
- Expert: “A clinical 74 points. The Salah captaincy was textbook — home fixture, set piece duty, form convergence. Your mini-league should be worried.”
- Critic: “67 points and you benched the highest scorer in the game. Honestly, at this point I’m impressed by your commitment to chaos.”
- Fanboy: “EIGHTY-TWO POINTS?! BRO. You are LITERALLY the main character this week. Screenshot this. Frame it. Name your firstborn after your captain. 🔥”
1. **A subtle call-to-action** — small text at the bottom: “superscout.pro” or “Get your coach at superscout.pro”. Not aggressive. Just enough that someone seeing the card on Twitter knows where it came from.

-----

## THE CARD DESIGN

The card must be designed as a VISUAL ASSET — something people are proud to share. Think of it like a social media graphic, not a data table.

**Dimensions:** 1080 x 1350 pixels (4:5 ratio). This is the optimal size for Instagram, looks great on Twitter/X, and renders well in WhatsApp and iMessage previews. If this ratio causes problems, 1080 x 1080 (1:1 square) is the fallback.

**Background:** Dark background (deep navy or charcoal — something like #1A1A2E or #16213E). White or light text. This looks premium and stands out in social feeds where most content has white backgrounds.

**Typography:** Clean, modern sans-serif. Player names should be readable even when the image is viewed at thumbnail size on a phone.

**The formation layout** is the centrepiece. Arrange the 11 players in their actual positions — goalkeeper at the bottom, forwards at the top (or vice versa — pick whichever looks better). Each player shows as a small card or badge with their surname and points. The captain’s card should be visually distinct (gold border, slightly larger, or a captain icon).

**The AI quip** should have its own visual treatment — perhaps in a slightly different font or in a speech-bubble style area — so it stands out as the personality element.

**The overall feel** should be: premium, clean, social-media-native. Not cluttered. Not cheap. Someone sharing this on Twitter should feel like it makes them look good.

## HOW IT WORKS — USER FLOW

1. User navigates to the Squad Card screen (a new tab or accessible from the post-gameweek Report Card flow).
1. The app pulls the user’s gameweek picks from the FPL API: `/entry/{manager_id}/event/{event_id}/picks/` for the squad and formation, and `/event/{event_id}/live/` for the actual points scored.
1. The app also pulls the user’s overall rank and rank movement from `/entry/{manager_id}/history/`.
1. The app sends the squad data, points, and rank to the AI (Claude API) along with the user’s chosen vibe prompt to generate the quip. The AI prompt for the quip should include:
- The user’s squad and points
- Their captain and captain points
- Their overall rank movement
- Their bench points (especially if a bench player outscored a starter — comedy gold for the Critic)
- The gameweek average score (from the outcomes table or FPL API) for comparison
- Instruction: generate ONE line (max 280 characters) in the specified vibe voice. Keep it specific to THIS gameweek’s results — not generic.
1. The card is rendered on screen as a React Native view.
1. The user sees a preview of their card with two buttons: **“Share”** and **“Save”**.
1. **Share** opens the native share sheet (React Native’s Share API or `expo-sharing`). This lets them share to Twitter/X, WhatsApp, iMessage, Instagram Stories, or any other app on their phone. The card is shared as an image file, not a link.
1. **Save** saves the card image to their device’s photo library (using `expo-media-library`).
1. Regardless of whether they share, save, or dismiss — the card generation is logged to the database.

## AI QUIP GENERATION

Use the existing vibe prompt system (the shared prompts in `config/vibes/vibePrompts.ts`). Add a specific instruction for quip generation. The system prompt stays the same (to maintain vibe consistency), but the user message should be structured like:

```
Generate a Squad Card quip for this gameweek. ONE sentence only, maximum 280 characters. Be specific to the actual results — reference the captain pick, standout performers, or bench disasters. Make it feel personal and shareable.

Gameweek: [number]
Total points: [points]
Captain: [player name] ([points] pts, [ownership]% owned)
Vice captain: [player name] ([points] pts)
Top scorer in squad: [player name] ([points] pts)
Highest bench scorer: [player name] ([points] pts)
Gameweek average: [average] pts
Rank movement: [up/down] [number] places
New overall rank: [rank]
```

The AI should return just the quip text — no preamble, no quotes, just the line.

**Important:** Use `max_tokens: 200` for quip generation. This is a single line, not a full recommendation. Keep AI costs minimal for this feature.

## IMAGE GENERATION FROM THE CARD VIEW

The card is built as a styled React Native component. To turn it into a shareable image:

1. Use `react-native-view-shot` (or `expo-view-shot` if available in Expo) to capture the card component as a PNG image.
1. The captured image is saved to a temporary file.
1. That file is what gets shared via the share sheet or saved to the photo library.

Make sure the captured image is high resolution (the full 1080 x 1350 or 1080 x 1080 — not a screen-resolution thumbnail).

## DATABASE LOGGING — TABLE 10 (squad_cards)

Every time a card is generated, write a row to the `squad_cards` table. This table already exists in Supabase from Phase 0. The fields to populate:

- **user_id** — the current user’s ID
- **season** — e.g. “2026-27”
- **gameweek** — the gameweek number this card was generated for
- **skin_used** — for now, always “default”. Premium skins come later. Store it as a string.
- **shared** — boolean. Set to `false` when the card is generated. Update to `true` if the user taps Share (regardless of whether they actually complete the share — we can’t track that on most platforms, so tapping the share button counts).
- **share_platform** — string, nullable. If we can detect which app they shared to from the share sheet result, store it (e.g. “twitter”, “whatsapp”, “imessage”, “clipboard”). If we can’t detect it, store “unknown”. If they didn’t share, leave null.
- **quip_text** — the full AI-generated quip text. Store it even if they don’t share. This is valuable data for analysing which quips correlate with sharing behaviour.
- **created_at** — timestamp, auto-generated.

## EDGE CASES TO HANDLE

- **Gameweek not yet finished:** If the user tries to generate a card for a gameweek that’s still in progress (matches haven’t all been played), show a message: “This gameweek isn’t finished yet. Come back after the last match to see your full results.” Don’t generate a partial card — it’ll look wrong when shared.
- **User hasn’t set a team:** If the user didn’t play that gameweek (no picks found), show: “No squad data found for this gameweek. Did you forget to set your team? The [vibe] has thoughts…”
- **AI quip fails:** If the Claude API call fails, use a fallback quip: “Gameweek [X] — [points] points. The numbers speak for themselves.” Don’t block the card generation because of an AI failure.
- **First gameweek (no rank history):** If there’s no previous rank to compare, show “New entry — Rank #[X]” instead of a rank movement arrow.
- **Zero points or very low score:** The AI should handle these gracefully. The Critic should be funny but not cruel. The Fanboy should be relentlessly optimistic. The Expert should be analytical about what went wrong.

## WHAT THIS FEATURE DOES NOT INCLUDE (YET)

- **Premium skins** — custom visual themes for the card. These come post-GW10 if the decision gate shows cards are being shared organically. The `skin_used` field is ready for this.
- **Animated cards** — video or GIF versions. Future feature. Static image for now.
- **Mini-league comparison cards** — showing your score vs a rival. This could be a Phase 2 addition tied to the Banter Engine.
- **Historical card gallery** — browsing your past cards. The data is being logged, so this can be built later from existing data.
